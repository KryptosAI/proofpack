Privacy Policy

Last Updated: July 2026

ProofPack ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use the ProofPack Stripe App and related SaaS platform (the "Service"). By using the Service, you agree to the practices described in this policy.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Information You Provide to Us

  • Stripe Account Information: When you connect your Stripe account via OAuth, we receive account identifiers, merchant name, contact email, and related account metadata necessary to interact with the Stripe API on your behalf. We do not receive or store your Stripe account password or full API secret keys entered directly by you (though we do store the access tokens and scoped API keys issued by Stripe OAuth for ongoing integration).
  • Merchant Profile: Your name, email address, and business information provided during registration or account setup.
  • Dispute Data: Chargeback records, dispute IDs, reason codes, amounts, associated payment intent metadata, and related evidence fetched from Stripe when a dispute is detected.

1.2 Information Collected Automatically

When you or your authorized users interact with the Service, we automatically collect:

  • Proof Events: A tamper‑evident audit trail that records user sign‑ins, acceptance of terms and conditions, credit or token consumption, AI‑generated output generation, file downloads, and other account actions relevant to dispute defense.
  • Technical Identifiers: IP addresses, device identifiers, session identifiers, browser type, operating system, and approximate geolocation derived from IP.
  • Usage Data: Pages or features accessed, timestamps of actions, and interaction patterns within the Service.

1.3 Information We Do NOT Collect

  • End‑customer payment details (such as full credit card numbers, CVC codes, or bank account numbers). This information remains within Stripe and is never transmitted to or stored by ProofPack.
  • Personal financial information of your end‑customers beyond what Stripe includes in a dispute record (e.g., last four digits of a card, billing address associated with the charge).

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect solely for the following purposes:

  • Dispute Evidence Generation: Compiling structured evidence packets — including proof events, timestamps, IP logs, device fingerprints, and AI‑output records — that you can submit to Stripe (and ultimately to the card network) in response to a chargeback.
  • Fraud Analysis and Dispute Defense: Correlating proof events with disputed transactions to identify patterns that support a compelling defense against fraudulent or erroneous chargebacks.
  • Service Delivery and Operations: Authenticating your account, processing API requests, monitoring system performance, debugging issues, and enforcing our terms of service.
  • Communication: Sending administrative messages, dispute alerts, and (where opted in) product updates. You may opt out of non‑administrative communications at any time.
  • Aggregated Analytics: We may use de‑identified, aggregated data for internal business intelligence and product improvement. This data cannot be linked back to you or any individual end‑customer.

We do not sell, rent, lease, or otherwise share your information with third parties. We do not use your data for cross‑context behavioral advertising or any purpose beyond those listed above.

3. Data Storage and Security

  • Storage: All data is stored on encrypted, access‑controlled infrastructure using industry‑standard cloud providers. Proof events and dispute records are persisted in secure databases with encryption at rest and in transit (TLS 1.2 or higher).
  • Access Controls: Access to production data is limited to the ProofPack developer (the "sole developer") and is protected by multi‑factor authentication, role‑based restrictions, and audit logging. No employee or contractor has unfettered access to raw customer data.
  • Security Measures: We employ reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards — including firewalls, intrusion detection, regular vulnerability assessments, and secure coding practices — to protect your information against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction.
  • Breach Notification: In the event of a data breach that affects your information, we will notify you without undue delay and in accordance with applicable law.

No method of electronic transmission or storage is 100% secure. While we strive to protect your data, we cannot guarantee absolute security.

4. Third‑Party Services

ProofPack integrates with the following third‑party services. These integrations are subject to the privacy policies of each respective provider, and we encourage you to review them.

4.1 Stripe (Required Integration)

ProofPack is a Stripe App and relies on Stripe's API to retrieve dispute data and submit evidence on your behalf. Stripe acts as a data processor with respect to payment and dispute information. Stripe's own privacy practices govern all information that remains within Stripe's systems. ProofPack does not duplicate or independently store end‑customer payment card details. For more information, see Stripe's Privacy Policy.

4.2 SendGrid (Optional)

If you enable email alerts, we use SendGrid (a Twilio service) to deliver transactional notifications, such as dispute alerts or evidence‑packet readiness notices. We share only the minimum information required to send these emails (your contact email address and the content of the notification). SendGrid's data processing is governed by its own privacy policy and Twilio's Data Protection Addendum.

4.3 Slack (Optional)

If you opt to receive Slack notifications, we use the Slack API to deliver messages to your designated Slack workspace channel. We share only the text content of the notification; we do not transmit personal data beyond what is necessary to route the message. Slack's privacy practices are available at Slack's Privacy Policy.

4.4 No Other Third‑Party Sharing

Apart from the integrations described above, ProofPack does not share, disclose, or transfer your information to any other third party. We do not use third‑party analytics, advertising networks, or data brokers.

5. Data Retention

  • Active Accounts: We retain your information for as long as your account remains active or as necessary to provide the Service. You may request deletion of your account and associated data at any time (see Section 6).
  • Inactive or Closed Accounts: After you close your account, we will delete or anonymize your data within 60 days, except where a longer retention period is required by law, or where data is necessary for our legitimate business purposes (such as defending against legal claims, enforcing our terms, or maintaining the integrity of dispute evidence records already submitted to Stripe).
  • Dispute Evidence: Evidence packets submitted to Stripe become subject to Stripe's retention policies and may persist in card‑network records. ProofPack cannot delete data that has already been transmitted to Stripe or a card network.

6. Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have certain rights regarding your personal information.

6.1 General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) — EEA / UK Residents

If you are located in the European Economic Area or the United Kingdom, you have the right to:

  • Request access to, rectification of, or erasure of your personal data.
  • Restrict or object to processing of your personal data.
  • Data portability (receive your data in a structured, machine‑readable format).
  • Withdraw consent at any time, where processing is based on consent.
  • Lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority.

6.2 California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) — California Residents

If you are a California resident, you have the right to:

  • Know what personal information we collect, use, disclose, or sell (we do not sell personal information).
  • Request deletion of your personal information.
  • Opt out of the sale or sharing of your personal information (again, we do not sell or share data).
  • Not be discriminated against for exercising any of these rights.

6.3 Exercising Your Rights

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at hello@proofpack.dev. We will respond to verified requests within the timeframes required by applicable law — typically 30 days for GDPR and 45 days for CCPA, subject to extensions where permitted. We may need to verify your identity before processing your request.

6.4 Authorized Agents

You may designate an authorized agent to submit requests on your behalf. We may require written proof of the agent's authorization and may still need to verify your identity directly.

Your continued use of the Service after the effective date of a revised policy constitutes your acceptance of the changes. If you do not agree with the updated policy, you must discontinue use of the Service and close your account.
